Regional Coach Support Grant

Tennis NSW and Tennis Country are committed to fostering a strong, sustainable tennis community across regional NSW. As part of this commitment, we are proud to offer the Regional Coach Support Grant, a targeted initiative designed to support the education and development of tennis coaches living and working in regional areas across the state.

Key grant details:

  • The Regional Coach Support Grant is a rolling grant, meaning applications are open 365 days of the year, allowing individuals to apply at any time that suits their development journey.
  • This flexible approach is designed to accommodate the varying schedules and needs of regional coaches.

Funding Available:

  • A maximum of $500.00 (AUD) is available per successful applicant.
  • The funding may be used to cover course fees or associated costs such as travel, accommodation, and coaching equipment related to attending the course.

Course Types:

  • To qualify for funding, applicants must be enrolled in one of the following accredited coaching qualifications by Tennis Australia:
    • Level 1 – Junior Development Coach
    • Level 2 – Club Professional Coach
    • Level 3 – Master Club Professional Coach

*Note: Courses classified as trainee, introductory, or participation coaching courses (including short workshops and non-accredited programs) are not eligible for this grant.

Application Timing:

  • The grant is open year-round, with applications accepted on a rolling basis.
  • Applications will be reviewed periodically by Tennis NSW, and successful applicants will be notified via email.

Payment of Funds

  • Grant funds will be paid directly to the successful applicant or, in some cases, to the course provider on behalf of the applicant.
  • Documentation such as proof of enrolment, course fee invoices, or receipts for eligible expenses may be required before funds are disbursed.

For more information, please view the Regional Coach Support Grant Guidelines here or contact your local Tennis Development Officer.
