Tennis Venues Across NSW Champion Inclusion During Inaugural All Abilities Week

6 November 2025 | Tennis NSW

Last month, Tennis Australia launched the inaugural All Abilities Week, a new initiative celebrating inclusion in tennis and creating more opportunities for people with disability both on and off the court.

Across NSW, clubs including Ulladulla Tennis Centre, Collaroy Tennis Club, City Community Tennis and Voyager Tennis Willoughby hosted events showcasing their inclusive programs and celebrating the spirit of accessible tennis. Keep reading to discover how venues across the state came together to make All Abilities Week a hit!

Elsewhere across NSW, Beaton Park Tennis Centre in Wollongong hosted a fantastic event that combined All Abilities Week with their Rally4Ever program, drawing strong community involvement and positive feedback from participants. City Community Tennis also held an inclusive event at Prince Alfred Park in Surry Hills, attracting around 45 attendees for an afternoon of fun, connection and adaptive play.

As part of Tennis NSW’s ongoing partnership with Sport4All, local clubs received support to enhance their practical understanding of inclusion and accessibility. Together, they delivered on-ground activities and an inclusive learning workshop with Collaroy Tennis Club. To find out more about Sport 4 All and how they can support your tennis venue CLICK HERE.
